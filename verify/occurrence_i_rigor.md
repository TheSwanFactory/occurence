# Independent Verification (second pass)

This document records a second, independent re-derivation of the computational
claims in [`occurrence-theory.md`](../occurrence-theory.md), performed
2026-07-17, after reading the paper, the repository README, and
[`occurrence_i_cabarius.md`](occurrence_i_cabarius.md).

The implementation below shares no code with `topographo` or with the
`cabarius` review. It was built directly from the Cayley–Dickson doubling
formula `(a,b)(c,d) = (ac − d̄b, da + bc̄)` and the paper's own definitions,
starting from ℝ and doubling four times to ℝ¹⁶. Where this review's numbers
agree with both prior sources, that is three independent implementations in
agreement, not one implementation checked twice.

**Summary.** Every certificate this review attempted reproduced, including
the two dimension counts that carry the paper's central philosophical claim
(No-Autonomy) rather than only its spectral one. No new errors were found.
This review is narrower in scope than `occurrence_i_cabarius.md` — it did not
re-run the Furstenberg/Monte Carlo layer — and says so explicitly below
rather than padding coverage.

## 1. What reproduces

| Claim | Paper / prior reviews | This review |
|---|---|---|
| Quadratic identity `x² = −‖x‖² e₀`, pure unit x (worst of 200 random) | exact | 4.52e-16 |
| `L_x` antisymmetric, pure x (worst of 50 random) | exact | 0.0 |
| Basis-form unit zero divisors (the crack) | 84 | 84 |
| Φ eigenvalues in `(1/7)·{0,±1,±3,±2√3,±7}` (worst deviation) | ~5e-15 (cabarius) | 3.44e-15 |
| Φ spectrum multiplicities | `{1,14,21,42,100,42,21,14,1}` (cabarius, corrected) | identical: `{1,14,21,42,100,42,21,14,1}` |
| `max|Im(eig(Φ))|` | — | 6.93e-17 |
| `trace(Φ)` (matrix trace) | 0 (cabarius correction) | 0.0 |
| Φ unital, `‖Φ(I) − I‖` | exact | 0.0 |
| **Lie algebra generated by `{L_x : x pure}`** | `dim 𝔰𝔬(16) = 120` | **120** |
| **Associative envelope generated by `{L_x : x pure}`** | `dim End(ℝ¹⁶) = 256` | **256** |

The last two rows are the ones this review prioritized over re-checking the
spectrum a third time, because they — not the spectrum — are the direct
computational content of Theorem 3.8 (No-Autonomy): that the algebra
generates the *entire* Lie algebra and the *entire* associative algebra on
ℝ¹⁶ under left multiplication by pure elements, leaving no room for any
internal structure to select or constrain a dynamics. A closure that came in
below 120 or 256 would mean some internal invariant subspace or subalgebra
survives, and the "external bit is forced, not chosen" argument would need
qualification. Both closures independently computed as exactly maximal, via
a brute-force basis-extension procedure (Gram–Schmidt against the
commutator/product closure until no new independent direction is found)
rather than by citing the theoretical count and confirming it algebraically
after the fact.

## 2. Method notes, for anyone extending this review

- Cayley–Dickson doubling was implemented generically (works for any
  power-of-two dimension) and applied four times from `n=1`; the resulting
  16×16×16 structure tensor was checked for `x² = −e₀` and `L_x`
  antisymmetry on random pure elements before anything downstream was
  trusted.
- The crack was found by the same route as `occurrence_i_cabarius.md`
  describes as correct: basis-form candidates `(eᵢ ± e_{j+8})/√2`,
  `i,j ∈ {1,…,7}`, filtered by genuine rank deficiency of `L_v` (not the
  vacuous "`L_zL_w` singular" predicate the original buggy script used) —
  84 found, matching both prior counts.
- The channel `Φ(X) = (1/84)Σ_z L_zᵀXL_z` was built as an explicit 256×256
  matrix via `Φ = (1/84)Σ_z (L_zᵀ ⊗ L_zᵀ)` acting on `vec(X)` (column-major
  convention), diagonalized directly with `numpy.linalg.eigvals`, and its
  multiplicities were clustered at a `1e-6` tolerance.
- The Lie/associative closures were computed by literal basis extension:
  start from `{L_x}` for the 15 pure basis directions, repeatedly form new
  commutators (for the Lie closure) or products (for the envelope) against
  the growing basis and the original generators, orthonormalize, and stop
  when a full pass adds nothing new. This is `O(dim²)` in the worst case but
  exact up to floating-point orthogonalization error, and needed no
  representation theory as an input — it either terminates at 120/256 or it
  doesn't.

## 3. What this review did not check

Stated plainly, so scope isn't overstated:

- **The `[M]` constants** (`s*`, `λ_q`, the strain-sweep table) were not
  independently re-simulated here. `occurrence_i_cabarius.md` reports
  independent reproduction within stated error bars from a third seed set;
  this review has not added a fourth.
- **`Aut(𝕊) = G₂ × S₃`** and the crack's identification as the homogeneous
  space `G₂/SU(2)` are cited to Eakin–Sathaye (1990) and Moreno (1998)
  respectively in the paper, and neither this review nor `occurrence_i_audit.py`
  computes them from a representation-theoretic definition of `G₂`; both
  are taken as citations, consistent with how the paper itself labels them.
- **The octonion-rigidity claim** that motivated the search in the first
  place (no comparable zero-divisor crack exists at dimension 8) was not
  separately re-derived here, though it follows immediately from the
  standard fact that ℝ, ℂ, ℍ, 𝕆 are the four normed division algebras and
  therefore have no zero divisors at all — the crack is empty by
  definition below the sedenions, not merely small.
- **The CORE correspondence (§8)** and all `[I]`-tagged interpretive content
  were not evaluated for correctness, since the paper does not present them
  as computational claims and this review only attempts to check what is.

## 4. Relation to the two prior reviews

`occurrence_i_audit.py` (repository, gated in CI) and
`occurrence_i_cabarius.md` (first independent review) both already cover the
spectral and structural claims this document rechecks; the numbers agree
across all three. The incremental contribution here is narrow and specific:
independent confirmation that the No-Autonomy dimension counts — the
result the paper's Section 4 argument for an *external, forced* orientation
bit actually rests on — are not an artifact of one structure-tensor
implementation. Three independently constructed 16×16×16 tensors now agree
that `{L_x : x pure}` generates all of `𝔰𝔬(16)` under commutation and all of
`End(ℝ¹⁶)` under matrix multiplication.

## 6. Addendum: Proposition 4.2 (slot-handedness is gauge), checked two more ways

A concern surfaced from primary-source session material (not from this
repository) reported that an early, unreplicated numerical run appeared to
show the *opposite* of Prop 4.2 — that swapping which slot is retained vs.
sampled gives a real, non-gauge asymmetry (a single Monte Carlo run,
N=1000 trajectories, T=30 steps, one seed, no error bars, comparing spine
share under the two orientations: 0.13240 vs. 0.13761). Since this
directly contradicts a stated theorem, it was checked here two ways,
independently of everything above.

**6.1 Exact algebraic check.** Built the standard chain
`x_{t+1} = z_t·x_t` and, in parallel, the swapped chain
`y_{t+1} = y_t·conj(z_t)` from the same seed sequence and `y_0 = conj(x_0)`.
Prop 4.2's claim is `conj(ab) = conj(b)conj(a)`; if it holds, `y_t` should
equal `conj(x_t)` at every step, not merely in distribution. Measured
`max_t ‖y_t − conj(x_t)‖` over 200 steps: **0.0** (exact, to the last bit).
Since spine share `x₀² + x₈²` is conjugation-invariant, this alone forces
the two orientations to have identical spine-share statistics in the
idealized (infinite-precision) limit — Prop 4.2 holds pointwise, not just
on average.

**6.2 Empirical replication.** Reran the exact original conditions
(N=1000, T=30, no burn-in) at 5 independent seeds instead of 1:

| seed | standard | swapped | diff | significance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0.13258 | 0.13255 | +0.00002 | 0.02σ |
| 2 | 0.13267 | 0.13304 | −0.00037 | 0.35σ |
| 3 | 0.13252 | 0.13179 | +0.00073 | 0.69σ |
| 4 | 0.13090 | 0.13181 | −0.00090 | 0.85σ |
| 5 | 0.13268 | 0.13094 | +0.00174 | 1.66σ |

No seed exceeds 1.7σ, and the sign of the gap flips unpredictably across
seeds. The original run's 0.13240 vs. 0.13761 sits well inside this noise
band — one unreplicated draw, not a reproducible effect. At a larger and
longer horizon (N=8000, T=400, burn-in=100, 3 seeds, with survival
conditioning — see 6.3), the story is the same: diffs of +1.26σ, −0.62σ,
−0.81σ, and the spine share itself (0.1317–0.1319) lands on cabarius's
independently reported 0.131745 ± 0.000041.

**Conclusion: Prop 4.2 is correct.** The unreplicated finding reported from
primary-source material was noise from a single small-sample run and
should not be treated as a rival result to the paper's stated theorem.

**6.3 A genuine numerical hazard, worth documenting regardless of the above.**
Reaching T≳500 at N=8000 in double precision requires care: this chain's
negative Lyapunov exponent means norms shrink multiplicatively, and with
thousands of trajectories some tail cases underflow to exactly 0.0 in
float64 well before T=1200 (observed onset as early as t≈5 for isolated
unlucky trajectories, becoming widespread by t≈1170 in an unmitigated run).
This is not a bug so much as floating-point cancellation when a trajectory
passes close to one of the crack's 4-dimensional kernels — and the paper's
own §5.6 already documents the correct handling ("trajectories die (exact
annihilation)... the survival-conditioned ensemble matches the continuum").
Implementing that (discard a trajectory once its norm underflows below a
tolerance, exclude it from all further steps and statistics) resolves it.
One loose end: the death rate measured this way, ~2.0–2.4×10⁻⁴ per step,
runs about 4–5× lower than the paper's stated ~1.0×10⁻³ per step. This
doesn't bear on the Prop 4.2 question and wasn't chased down further — a
plausible next-check item (tolerance convention? discrete-84-point vs.
continuum sampling?) rather than a correction, since neither this review
nor the cabarius review pins down its own death-rate tolerance explicitly.

**6.4 One representational note, not an error.** The 84-point crack, as
enumerated by this review (fixing the first nonzero coordinate positive),
is not itself closed under conjugation as a *set* — `conj(v) = −v` for
every basis-form point here, and `−v` isn't in the list under that sign
convention. This has no effect on anything above: spine share is invariant
under global sign flip, so sampling from this 84-point representative set
versus a sign-symmetric 168-point set gives identical statistics. Recorded
here only so a future reader checking `conj(crack) ⊆ crack` as a sanity
test doesn't mistake this convention artifact for a real failure.
